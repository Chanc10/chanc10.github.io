About Me

Hello! My name is Chandler Crisp, I am a Computer Science major at UNC Charlotte and I love playing video games. I can remember first picking up a GameBoy when I was five years old, and from that day on I knew I would love gaming. This site will explore five of my favorite games and franchises. Over the years I have owned many different consoles, played a ton of games, and made lifelong friendships with people I never knew I would meet!

The GameCube and GameBoy were 2 of the first consoles I played as a child. Both of these have an icredible library of classic Nintendo games!

The Legend of Zelda

The Legend of Zelda is one of my all time favorite video game franchises. While there are many entries on many different platforms, most of them follow the same structure: Link, a commoner, is often thrown into a mysterious quest in the kingdom of Hyrule to save the princess, Zelda, from some form of evil, the most common being Ganondorf.

This is a collage of the many iterations of Link throughout the years.

Call of Duty

Call of Duty was the first shooter franchise that I got into as a kid. There have been many installments by different developers with different characters and plots. However, all games are still a first-person shooter with a somewhat similar feel. My favorite game in this series would have to be Call of Duty: Black Ops 3, which released in 2015.

This is a picture of the cover art for Call of Duty: Black Ops 3

Minecraft

Minecraft is one of the bestselling video games of all time, and for good reason. In this building block game, you can do pretty much whatever you can think of. This game released in 2011 and became one of the most played games in the world around 2014, and it is still at that status today.

In Minecraft you can create massive structures from just one block, like the castle pictured above.

Rocket League

Rocket League is the game that I am currently having the most fun with, probably because it combines two of my favorite things: soccer and cars! Rocket League is a soccer game, but you are driving a car instead of controlling a player. You can boost up in the air, drive up the wall, and hit crazy pinch shots with other cars.

Valorant

Valorant is an FPS style shooter that released in the summer of 2020. I started playing this game when it came out because I was super bored with covid and everything keeping me inside a lot. At first I hated this game because it has a very large skill gap and it takes a bit of grinding to figure eveything out. With that being said, I still enjoy this game a lot and I try to play it when I have some free time.
